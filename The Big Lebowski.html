The Big Lebowski

About The Big Lebowski

The Big Lebowski is a 1998 cult comedy by the Coen Brothers. It follows "The Dude" (Jeff Bridges), a laid-back slacker drawn into a convoluted kidnapping plot after a case of mistaken identity. Known for its quirky characters, memorable quotes, and surreal humor, the film has a lasting fanbase.

Characters

Jeffrey "The Big" Lebowski

Jeffrey "The Big" Lebowski, also known as "The Dude," is the iconic main character from the 1998 film The Big Lebowski, directed by the Coen brothers. Played by Jeff Bridges, he is a laid-back, eccentric man living in Los Angeles who becomes entangled in a complex kidnapping case after being mistaken for a millionaire with the same last name. Known for his carefree philosophy, love of bowling, and his drink of choice—the White Russian—The Dude has become a cultural symbol of relaxation and nonconformity.

Walter Sobchak

Walter Sobchak is a main character from The Big Lebowski, portrayed by John Goodman. He is a Vietnam War veteran and best friend of the film's protagonist, "The Dude." Walter is known for his intense, over-the-top personality, rigid adherence to rules (especially regarding bowling), and frequent outbursts. His obsession with his past military service and strong opinions often escalate conflicts in humorous and absurd ways. Despite his flaws, Walter is fiercely loyal and remains a memorable, larger-than-life figure in the film.
