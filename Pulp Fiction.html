Pulp Fiction

About Pulp Fiction

Pulp Fiction is a 1994 American crime film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino, who conceived it with Roger Avary. Starring John Travolta, Samuel L. Jackson, Bruce Willis, Tim Roth, Ving Rhames, and Uma Thurman, it tells several stories of criminal Los Angeles. The title refers to the pulp magazines and hardboiled crime novels popular during the mid-20th century, known for their graphic violence and punchy dialogue.

Characters

Vincent Vega

Vincent Vega is a main character in Pulp Fiction, portrayed by John Travolta. He is a suave, charismatic hitman who works for mob boss Marsellus Wallace. Vincent is known for his cool demeanor, slick dance moves, and philosophical conversations with his partner, Jules Winnfield. Despite his criminal lifestyle, Vincent is shown to have a sensitive side and struggles with personal issues, such as his drug addiction and romantic entanglements. His iconic portrayal by Travolta helped revive the actor's career and solidified the character as a pop culture icon.

Jules Winnfield

Jules Winnfield is a hitman in Pulp Fiction, played by Samuel L. Jackson. He is known for his intimidating presence, sharp wit, and philosophical musings on morality and redemption. Jules undergoes a spiritual transformation after surviving a near-death experience, leading him to question his violent lifestyle and seek a path of righteousness. His iconic "Ezekiel 25:17" speech and distinctive hairstyle have made him one of the most memorable characters in film history.
