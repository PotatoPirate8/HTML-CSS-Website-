Minesweeper

About Minesweeper

Minesweeper is a classic computer game that involves clearing a minefield without detonating any mines. The game is played on a grid of squares, some of which contain hidden mines. The player must use logic to determine the locations of the mines and mark them with flags. The objective is to clear all non-mine squares without triggering any mines.

Minesweeper techniques

Flagging

Flagging is a key strategy in Minesweeper that involves marking squares that are suspected to contain mines. By flagging squares, players can keep track of potential mine locations and avoid detonating them accidentally. Flagging is essential for clearing the minefield efficiently and safely.

Chord Clicking

Chord clicking is a technique in Minesweeper that allows players to uncover multiple squares simultaneously. By clicking on a number square with the correct number of adjacent flagged squares, players can reveal all remaining unflagged squares around the number. Chord clicking is a useful strategy for clearing large areas of the minefield quickly.
